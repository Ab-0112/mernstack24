Bhagavatham

The Bhagavad Gita is a 700-verse Hindu scripture from the Indian epic Mahabharata, set in a conversation between Prince Arjuna and Lord Krishna. It provides philosophical guidance on life, duty, and the universe, focusing on concepts like dharma, karma, and moksha. The Gita emphasizes spiritual growth and self-realization, teaching individuals to integrate spirituality with life. It also provides personal counseling, emphasizing honesty and even-tempered behavior.
