सञ्जय उवाच
तं तथा कृपयाविष्टमश्रुपूर्णाकुलेक्षणम् ।
विषीदन्तमिदं वाक्यमुवाच मधुसूदनः ॥ १ ॥

Sañjaya said: Seeing Arjuna full of compassion, his mind depressed, his eyes full of tears, Madhusūdana, Kṛṣṇa, spoke the following words.

The Bhagavad Gita is a 700-verse Hindu scripture that is part of the Indian epic Mahabharata, specifically in the Bhishma Parva (Book of Bhishma). It is a spiritual dialogue between Prince Arjuna and the god Krishna, who serves as his charioteer. The conversation takes place on the battlefield of Kurukshetra just before the onset of a great war, where Arjuna is confused and morally troubled about fighting in the war. The Gita addresses his concerns and provides profound teachings on life, duty, and spirituality. The text is revered for its philosophical and practical guidance on living a meaningful life.

Bhagavad Gita Chapters

Chapter No. Chapter Name Summary
1 Arjuna Vishada Yoga Arjuna, filled with doubt and grief, surrenders to Krishna seeking guidance.
2 Sankhya Yoga Krishna explains the nature of the soul and duty. Karma Yoga and detachment from results are emphasized.
3 Karma Yoga The path of selfless action is outlined; performing one's duty without attachment is essential.
4 Jnana Karma Sanyasa Yoga Transcendental knowledge is revealed; Krishna discusses his divine incarnations.
5 Karma Sanyasa Yoga Comparison of renunciation and selfless action; Krishna recommends detached action.
6 Dhyana Yoga The practice of meditation is described; control of the mind and spiritual consciousness is discussed.
7 Gyaan Vigyana Yoga Krishna reveals his supreme nature and the illusionary power of Maya; four types of devotees are described.
8 Akshara Brahma Yoga The importance of remembering Krishna at the moment of death to attain liberation is emphasized.
9 Raja Vidya Yoga Krishna explains the supremacy of Bhakti and the importance of offering everything to God.
10 Vibhooti Yoga Krishna describes his divine manifestations and glories, revealing his omnipresence.
11 Vishwaroopa Darshana Yoga Krishna reveals his universal form to Arjuna, showcasing his divine power and vastness.
12 Bhakti Yoga Devotion (Bhakti) is presented as the highest spiritual path, and the qualities of a true devotee are described.
13 Ksetra Ksetrajna Vibhaaga Yoga The distinction between the body (field) and the soul (knower of the field) is explained.
14 Gunatraya Vibhaga Yoga Krishna discusses the three Gunas (goodness, passion, ignorance) and their effects on behavior.
15 Purushottama Yoga Krishna explains the transcendental nature of the Supreme Being and the process of liberation.
16 Daivasura Sampad Vibhaga Yoga The divine and demoniac natures of human beings are discussed, leading to spiritual growth or bondage.
17 Sraddhatraya Vibhaga Yoga Krishna describes the three types of faith and their connection to the modes of nature.
18 Moksha Sanyaas Yoga Renunciation and liberation are discussed, and Krishna emphasizes unconditional devotion to attain Moksha.
